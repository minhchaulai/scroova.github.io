Event Information

Washington DC Conference (October 30th-November 1st)

In fall semesters, we attend a 3 day National Model United Nations (NMUN) hosted conference in Washington, DC. Typically held the last weekend of October, over 850 students attend this conference each year. The relatively short conference length encourages delegates to quickly learn to cooperate and pass resolutions satisfactory to all parties.

CU High School Conference (Date TBA)

Each year, CUMUN hosts a conference at CU for regional highschoolers to participate in. Over 400 high school students attend to engage in a thoughtful debate about international issues guided by experienced Model UN members. This is an opportunity for CU students to teach the skills they've learned and give back to the community.

New York City Conference A (March 20th-24th)

The biggest conference of the spring semester, hosted by NMUN. Split into two separate weeks due to the sheer number of attendees, is the best conference for the ultimate Model UN experience. Delegates come from all over the world to participate in this week long session. With 4 whole days to explore the assigned topics, delegates can really dig in and fully explore complex solutions to the world's issues.

Speaker Event (Date TBA)

Model UN hosts a speaker event each year open to students to talk about interesting current events and issues. Last year's speaker was Colonel Bogdanos, an Iraq War veteran who lead an operation to recover hundreds of artifacts from the Iraq National Museum. This year's speaker and dates are to be announced.

Meeting Info

Our meetings are held in the Visual Arts Center (VAC) room 1B90 on Thursdays at 6:30pm.
